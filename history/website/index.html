About

Hello!


My name is Charles Hager and I'm a junior at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. I'm currently working as a developer for the Berkeley Haas School of Business, where I'm building a flexible template for creating web apps for professors using Bootstrap, Angular, Node, and Google scripts. When my time isn't taken up by classes or work, I like to pursure my passion for foreign languages; I speak English and French fluently and I've studied Latin, Chinese, and Japanese.


I've always been fascinated by computer science and I explore my interests in my personal projects in the summer or during lulls in the school year. I've spent a lot of time working with full-stack development, from a school project writing the backend of a Berkeley-focused "Google Maps" to my current job using front end and server side Javascript. I also have a huge passion for computer architecture and CPU design from my favorite class at UCB: CS 61c. I've worked on two CPU projects: one on my own to prepare for 61c and one in class that interpreted MIPS with a multi-stage pipeline.
